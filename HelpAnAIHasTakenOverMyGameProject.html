Help! An AI has taken over my game | 12/7/20

Still a bit ambitious. If I had to sum up this game in 4 words, thats what they would be. Actually, scrap that, ' way to ambitous' is how I'd some up this game! Its like I tried to create something bigger than my last game jam in one less day. While I did finish, looking back I'm not happy with the game and should of (once again) done something simpler. However, there are some positives and I think I have a strategy to make sure I don't make the same mistakes again.

This rather poorly named game (I was running out of time!) was made for GMTK 2020: Out of Control and you can check it out here. You have to help out an unfortunate game developer who has created an AI (well, not really) that has gotten a bit out of control and has taken over his game. As you, the player, are 'inside' the game you can help him get it back.

I think the overall concept of the game is pretty cool and at least on paper fits the theme quite well. However, thats about where the positives end. Once again I tried to do too much in the time frame. If I want to make a large level, have a storyline, create all the art myself, etc then something (or preferably multiple things) need to be cut or everything suffers. I could go on, but just imagine a less than medicore platformer. I think the game will rank poorly and quite frankly deserves to, however it will still be good to get some feedback as people will likely point out flaws I missed - helping me improve.

Despite this, I did have a couple of positive takeaways. Firstly, my standard of code was a lot higher than last time (except towards the very end) and secondly I really liked the grapple mechanic. If I could go back, I think I would create an entire game based around this mechanic. I've put it up on Github here incase I ever need it again.

Anyway, I hope this is the last time I will have to be taught the lesson of 'don't try something huge in a game jam'. My plan to stop this in the future is to imagine that (at least in the planning stage) that I have one less day than I actually do. This way, hopefully, I'll design a smaller project and therefore it will be a lot better. I am looking forward to implementing this approach into a future jam as I have yet to have a submission I feel truly proud of.
